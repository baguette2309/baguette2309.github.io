About Plastic Pollution

Plastic pollution is a global issue. It is the accumulation of plastic objects and particles (like plastic bottles, bags, microbeads, etc.) in the environment that adversely affects wildlife, wildlife habitat, and people. These adverse effects to the world caused by plastic pollution can be seen here. Most plastics are not biodegradable and can persist in the environment for hundreds to thousands of years. Over the last 70 years, plastic production around the world has sharply increased. In 1950, the world produced only two million tonnes, but now it produces over 450 million tonnes. Every year, approximately 19-23 million tonnes of plastic waste leaks into aquatic ecosystems, polluting lakes, rivers and seas, and around 176-187 million tonnes of plastic are dumped into landfills. By 2050, there could be more plastic in the ocean than fish in the ocean (by weight). However, there are ways that you can help reduce plastic pollution, such as reducing, reusing, and recycling plastic, or by participating in local clean-ups.
